Southern Regions

13 June 2025 Admin
People and Languages

- Main groups: Motu-Koitabu, Huli, Orokaiva, Kiwai, Maisin
- Languages: Hiri Motu (used in trade), Kiwai, Toaripi, local dialects

13 June 2025 Admin
Traditions and Customs

- Hiri trade tradition: Long-distance sea trading using canoes
- Lagatoi canoes: Large traditional sailing canoes
- Hiri Moale Festival: Celebrates ancient Motuan trade culture
- Tattooing and body art: Especially among Gulf and Oro people

13 June 2025 Admin
Art and Performance

- Grass skirts, face painting, wooden masks
- Drumming, singing, and storytelling during ceremonies

New Guinea Islands Region

13 June 2025 Admin
People and Languages

- Main groups: Tolai, Baining, Sulka, Tinputz, Nalik, Bougainvilleans
- Languages: Mostly Austronesian, widely use Tok Pisin

13 June 2025 Admin
Traditions and Customs

- Matrilineal societies: Land and names passed through mothers
- Tubuan & Duk-Duk societies: Secret male groups for law and ceremony
- Baining fire dance: Fire-walking ceremonies with large masks

13 June 2025 Admin
Art and Performance

- Canoe carving, bark cloth, wood sculptures
- Traditional dances, singing, and large festivals

People and Languages

-The name "Momase" is a portmanteau of its constituent provinces: Morobe, Madang, Sepik (East and West)

13 June 2025 Admin
Traditions and Customs

A deep connection to the land and ancestral spirits is central to the traditions and customs of the Momase Region. Ceremonies, rituals, and spiritual practices play a vital role in community life, connecting people to their ancestors and the natural world

13 June 2025 Admin
Art and Performance

- Traditional dances and music are central to the region's cultural expression. Performances often involve elaborate costumes, face painting, and the use of traditional instruments. These events are not only a form of entertainment but also a means of preserving and passing down cultural knowledge to younger generations.

Highlands Region

13 June 2025 Admin
People and Languages

-Highlands Region of Papua New Guinea is known for its rugged terrain and rich cultural diversity. It is divided into seven provinces: Eastern Highlands, Simbu (Chimbu), Western Highlands, Southern Highlands, Enga, Hela, and Jiwaka
- Languages: Tok Pisin,Huli,Enga,Ipili,Duna and Foe

13 June 2025 Admin
Traditions and Customs

Sing-sings are traditional gatherings where different tribes come together to celebrate and showcase their cultural heritage through music, dance, and elaborate costumes.

13 June 2025 Admin
Art and Performance

Gerua (Ritual Boards): These brightly painted ceremonial boards are produced by various Highlands groups, particularly the Siane people of the Eastern Highlands.
