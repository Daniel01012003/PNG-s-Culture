About Papua New Guinea

Brief History

Papua New Guinea has a long, rich history, from ancient cultures to colonial rule, WWII battles, and independence. Its people are proud of their diversity, freedom, and nationality

Vision 2050

Vision 2050 was launched in 2009 as the government's long-term strategic vision for Papua New Guinea. The Vision is described as a forty-year strategy to map our future direction and guide the nation's socio economic development. The Vision includes the intention that all otter medium and short term government strategies and plans should align to the Vision.

Though equalization and broad-based development is suggested in the Vision there are no specific measures to phase out of the extreme dependence on the mining sector which wil therefore continue to compromise political sovereignty and economic self-reliance. The flag of Papua New Guinea is a striking and meaningful design. It was officially adopted on July 1, 1971 and features a diagonal split with two distinct sections: The upper red triangle showcases a yellow Raggiana bird-of-paradise, symbolizing the country's cultural heritage and independence. The lower black triangle contains the Southern Cross constellation, representing Papua New Guinea's geographical location in the Southern Hemisphere. The colors red and black have long been traditional in many Papua New Guinean tribes. The flag was designed by Susan Karike, a 15-year-old student, after a nationwide competition
